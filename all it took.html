All it took was just a little spark to

set me on fire. The heat from that passion

fueled my drive and gave me a whole new life.

One with sunny, blue, skies and chirping

blue jays, their melodies carried

by a breeze incensed with fragrant flowers.


But it swept out of my control, making

Of me its slave. It burnt flowers,

shrouded the sky with pungent smoke,

fueled my despair and drove the birds away.

And when it burnt out, for all things must pass

you see, it left me cold and numb.

After all, when savannah fires expire

Naught is left but dead, smoking ash
